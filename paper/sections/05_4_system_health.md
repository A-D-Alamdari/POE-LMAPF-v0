# §5.4  System Health Indicators

In configurations where throughput is task-arrival-limited, the
throughput column does not reflect agent-level progress.  The
`deadlock_count` column does.

Throughput on the warehouse map at $|M| \ge 100$ tracks the task-arrival
process: every released task is picked up within a few ticks and
completed before the next batch arrives, so throughput saturates near
the arrival rate regardless of how many agents are functionally
stuck.  A subset of agents safety-wait at corridor junctions for the
remainder of the run and contribute zero to throughput, yet the
`completed_tasks` counter -- and hence `throughput` -- stays near its
ceiling because the OTHER agents pick up the slack.  Without the
diagnostics in this section a reader of Tables 1 and 2 would see only
the throughput-saturation regime and conclude all $|M|$ agents are
making progress; in fact a third of the fleet is silently lost in
every high-density warehouse run.

Reading the table:

* At $|M| = 100$, the throughput column shows 0.396 (40% per tick).
  Reading throughput alone, the system is functioning.  The
  deadlock counter says **16.10 agents (mean across seeds) are
  permanently stuck** -- 16% of the fleet -- and **every seed has
  at least one deadlocked agent**.
* As $|M|$ grows, the deadlock count saturates around 20 (~13% of
  the fleet at $|M|=150$ ... ~8% at $|M|=250$).  The fraction of
  seeds with at least one deadlocked agent stays at 100% across
  every density.
* The `Max DL` column is the worst-seed deadlock count and reaches
  31 agents stuck simultaneously at $|M|=200$ and $|M|=250$ --
  roughly an eighth of the deployed fleet, unrecoverable for the
  rest of the 2000-step horizon.

## Cross-method comparison at $|M| = 100$ on the warehouse map

The same metric across the four §5.5 methods (source CSV
`logs/paper/baseline_comparison_v2/results.csv`):

| Method | Deadlock count (mean) | Max DL | Frac. with DL |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Ours (POE-LMAPF) | 16.10 | 22 | 100% |
| No-Buffer        | 29.30 | 35 | 100% |
| PIBT2-FR         | 43.00 | 56 | 100% |
| LaCAM-blind      | 100.00 | 100 | 100% |

`LaCAM-blind` deadlocks **every agent in every run** -- the rigid-
follower controller has no recovery path when the global solution
becomes infeasible mid-run, and the entire fleet ends up in a
safety-wait state.  The §5.5 throughput comparison shows
LaCAM-blind at 0.205 vs Ours at 0.396; reading throughput alone,
LaCAM-blind looks half-as-good.  Reading deadlock_count, it is
**catastrophically broken**: not a single agent makes progress
past mid-run.  This is the kind of regression `deadlock_count`
catches that `throughput` does not.

`No-Buffer` and `PIBT2-FR` sit between the two extremes:
deadlock counts at 29 and 43 respectively (vs 16 for Ours), again
100% of runs affected.  The Ours / No-Buffer comparison is
particularly telling: same throughput within 1%, but No-Buffer
loses 13 more agents per run than Ours does.
